Ingredients to avoid
High Risk
High Fructose Corn Syrup
Also: HFCS, Corn Syrup, Maize Syrup
Processed sugar that goes straight to your liver. Your body can't tell when it's full — so you keep eating.
In: Soft drinks, cereals, bread, condiments
High Risk
Red 40, Yellow 5 & 6
Also: Allura Red, Tartrazine, Sunset Yellow
Made from petroleum. Linked to hyperactivity in children. Banned in Europe but still used in Australia and the US.
In: Lollies, sports drinks, cereal
Moderate
Maltodextrin
Also: Modified Starch, Corn Maltodextrin
A processed starch that spikes blood sugar even faster than regular sugar. Damages gut bacteria.
In: Energy gels, protein powder, sauces
High Risk
BHA & BHT
Also: Butylated Hydroxyanisole, Hydroxytoluene
Synthetic preservatives. Classified as a possible carcinogen. Disrupts hormones.
In: Chips, cereal, chewing gum
Moderate
Aspartame
Also: E951, NutraSweet, Equal
Classified as "possibly cancer-causing" by the WHO in 2023. Despite zero calories, it can make you hungrier.
In: Diet drinks, sugar-free gum, protein bars
Moderate
Sodium Benzoate
Also: E211, Benzoic Acid
Reacts with Vitamin C to create benzene — a known carcinogen. Often found in vitamin-fortified drinks at the same time.
In: Juices, dressings, energy drinks
Seed oils — what to avoid and what to use instead
Avoid these
Canola Oil
Creates toxic compounds when heated. Usually genetically modified.
Soybean Oil
In almost every processed food. Causes inflammation and disrupts hormones.
Sunflower Oil
Breaks down under heat and creates harmful compounds.
Cottonseed Oil
A byproduct of the cotton industry — not a food crop.
Use these instead
Extra Virgin Olive Oil
Rich in healthy fats. Linked to heart health and longer life.
Butter & Ghee
Stable at high heat. Contains natural vitamins. How humans have cooked for centuries.
Coconut Oil
Very stable. Converts quickly to energy. Natural antimicrobial properties.
Avocado Oil
Great for cooking at high heat. Rich in healthy fats.
