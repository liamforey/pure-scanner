Toxic Ingredients — What To Avoid
High Fructose Corn Syrup
High Risk
HFCS · Corn Syrup · Maize Syrup
Industrially processed from corn starch. Fructose is metabolised entirely by the liver — promoting fat accumulation, insulin resistance, and fatty liver disease. Bypasses satiety hormones entirely.
Found in
Soft drinksCerealsCondimentsBread
Red 40 · Yellow 5 · Yellow 6
High Risk
Allura Red · Tartrazine · Sunset Yellow
Petroleum-derived synthetic dyes. Linked to hyperactivity in children. Banned or warning-labelled across the EU — still widespread in Australia and the US.
Found in
CandySports drinksMac & cheeseCereals
Maltodextrin
Moderate
Modified Starch · Corn Maltodextrin
Glycaemic index higher than table sugar. Alters gut bacteria and promotes harmful bacteria growth. Used as a cheap filler in sports nutrition and processed foods.
Found in
Energy gelsProtein powderSaucesDressings
Sodium Benzoate
Moderate
E211 · Benzoic Acid
Reacts with Vitamin C to form benzene — a Group 1 carcinogen. Many vitamin-fortified drinks contain both, generating this toxic compound in the bottle.
Found in
PicklesDressingsJuicesEnergy drinks
BHA & BHT
High Risk
Butylated Hydroxyanisole · Hydroxytoluene
Synthetic antioxidants used to prevent fats going rancid. BHA is a Group 2B possible carcinogen (IARC). Both are hormone disruptors shown to interfere with neurological development.
Found in
ChipsCerealChewing gumMeats
Carrageenan
Moderate
E407 · Irish Moss Extract
Triggers gut inflammation in multiple studies. Degraded forms are classified as possible carcinogens by the IARC. Particularly alarming in infant formula.
Found in
Plant milksIce creamDeli meatsFormula
Aspartame
Moderate
E951 · NutraSweet · Equal
Classified "possibly carcinogenic" by the WHO in 2023. Metabolises into methanol and aspartic acid. Linked to disrupted gut microbiome and paradoxically increased appetite.
Found in
Diet sodasSugar-free gumProtein bars
Titanium Dioxide
High Risk
E171 · CI 77891
Used to whiten foods. Nanoparticles damage DNA and disrupt gut microbiome. Banned in the EU since 2022. Still approved in Australia and the US.
Found in
Chewing gumCandyIcingSupplements
Polysorbate 80
Moderate
E433 · Tween 80
Disrupts gut microbiome, thins the intestinal mucus layer, promotes low-grade inflammation. Associated with inflammatory bowel disease in research models.
Found in
Ice creamBaked goodsSauces
Seed Oils — Pillar 03
Toxic Oils vs. Clean Alternatives
Industrialised seed oils make up 20% of calories in the Western diet — none existed before 1900
Avoid these
Canola Oil
Unstable fats that oxidise under heat, generating aldehydes linked to cancer and inflammation. Most is GMO, solvent-extracted with hexane.
Soybean Oil
Extreme omega-6 content destroys the ratio humans evolved on. Found in nearly every processed food. Linked to obesity and neurological dysfunction.
Sunflower & Corn Oil
Extremely high in linoleic acid. Oxidises rapidly at cooking temperatures. Oxidation products are directly cytotoxic to cells.
Cottonseed Oil
A byproduct of the cotton industry — not food. Contains natural toxins including gossypol. Introduced into food as industrial waste disposal.
Choose these
Extra Virgin Olive Oil
Rich in oleic acid and polyphenols. Stable at moderate heat. Decades of Mediterranean data linking EVOO to reduced cardiovascular disease and all-cause mortality.
Grass-fed Butter & Ghee
Saturated fats are heat-stable. Contains vitamins A, D, E, K2 and CLA. Butyrate supports gut lining integrity. The way humans have cooked for millennia.
Coconut Oil
Predominantly saturated — extremely heat-stable. MCTs convert rapidly to energy rather than stored as fat. Antimicrobial properties from lauric acid.
Avocado Oil
High smoke point (271°C). Rich in monounsaturated fats and lutein. Minimally processed compared to seed oils.

THE Grocery Vision
A physical store where every product passes The Five Pillars — no exceptions
01
Zero Seed Oils
Canola, soybean, sunflower, corn, cottonseed — banned entirely. Every product prepared with traditional fats humans have used for millennia.

Organic & Pasture-Raised
Sourced from farms prioritising soil health, animal welfare, and traditional methods. Certified organic where possible, verified transparent where not.
03
No Additives. Full Stop.
No artificial colours, flavours, preservatives, emulsifiers, or stabilisers. Preserved the way our ancestors preserved it — salt, fermentation, drying.
04
Radical Transparency
Every product links to its farm source, ingredient origin, and processing method. No misleading health claims. Full disclosure, always.
05
Education Built In
In-store education at every shelf. Why we chose this product, what makes it different, and what to look for when you shop elsewhere.
